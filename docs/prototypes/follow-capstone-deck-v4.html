01 · THE SETUP

The work isn't written anymore.
It's negotiated.

BEFORE LLMs
NOW
You wrote your own document. Grammar tools cleaned it. Paraphrasing tools tightened it. The reasoning was yours; the polish was assisted.

The artifact and its origin were the same person.
You open a chat. You think out loud. You push back, ask follow-ups, branch into tangents. You copy the parts that landed — or the model assembles the artifact for you.

The artifact arrives. It looks like a document. It reads cleanly. Technically yours.
The reasoning happened in dialogue. The artifact is the visible end of an invisible process.

02 · THE AI-SHAPED HOLE

An artifact arrives.
Its reasoning lives somewhere else.

You bring AI-assisted work to your team. The reasoning behind half of it lives in a chat session only you saw. You can describe what the artifact says. You may struggle to reconstruct how — what the AI suggested, what you rejected, what shifted your thinking.
MIT MEDIA LAB · 2025
"Cognitive debt"
LLM-assisted writers showed weaker neural connectivity, lower self-reported ownership, and struggled to accurately quote essays they had written minutes earlier. Effects persisted across sessions.
Kosmyna et al., arXiv:2506.08872
FROM PEER CONCEPT TESTING
"I really try to review what it gives me before I hand it in. I want to make sure I can say it in my own words."
The labor of re-internalising what the model gave you. The friction is not the AI's quality — the reasoning happened somewhere else.
A provenance hole — where the AI used to be.

03 · TRANSACTIVE MEMORY

Teams already remember together.

WEGNER · 1985 / 1987

Transactive memory.

Each person holds their own knowledge. Everyone knows who holds what. So when you need something you don't have, you don't search — you ask the right person.

The system ends up bigger than any one person inside it.

This is how teams already operate. With each other.

The team didn't fail. We just operated at lower fidelity than we could have.

05 · WHAT'S AT STAKE

"I could have done a better job."

The problem isn't urgent. It's slow, compounding — and it's where work is heading.
01

Better work

When you can see how a teammate got to their conclusion, you build on it instead of redoing it or working around it.

02

Less time searching

Instead of re-reading three chats and asking two people, you ask once and the directory points you to the right person and the right thread.

03

Less setup each session

Capture happens where you're already working. You stop pasting context into a new chat every time.

04

Where things are going

Most teams will be working this way in a couple of years. Starting now means the team learns the habit before the tooling forces it.

Honest framing. The Housing Works deliverable shipped — Sharka, Pani, and I didn't fail. But I could have done better work if I'd known how they got where they got. That gap shows up across projects, not just one.

09 / 22
07 · INSIGHTS

Three insights.

01

AI is the invisible teammate.

Teams already operate as transactive memory systems with each other. They don't operate that way with their AI — not because they refuse to share, but because the AI's contributions have not been captured in a form the team can route to.

Sources: Housing Works lived experience + Intelligence Gap workshop.
02

Cross-tool team memory is structurally vacant.

Native memory inside a single AI tool is solved by vendors. Cross-tool, cross-contributor memory is empty — and not accidentally. No vendor with the surface to build it has the commercial reason to make it cross-vendor.

Sources: industry expert interviews (Andy + Atanu) + competitive landscape.
03

Provenance matters most where consequence is high.

The strongest signal for value comes from contexts where the cost of getting it wrong is asymmetric: legal review, regulated knowledge work, compliance. These are also where pricing tolerance is highest.

Sources: outside-perspective interview + Atanu interview (vertical wedge).

DEMO 1 · STRATEGIC DESIGN TEAM

Three teammates. One shared project.

Scenario: A research project for a community housing client. Three team members, three AI tools, conversations & documents flowing into a shared Follow index.
"What has Sharka been working on this week?"
→ directory_query + query_index
Sharka's contributions surface, attributed. New framing about housing-first as a design constraint — traced to a source she read this morning.
"What changed in her thinking about housing-first this week?"
→ query_index(freshness: include_superseded)
Supersession chain returns. Monday's framing as superseded; Wednesday's framing as live. Both queryable.
"Is anything contested between me, Sharka, and Pani right now?"
→ detect_contradictions(scope: project)
Contradiction surfaces between Pani's earlier transitional-housing framing and Sharka's newer claim. Neither flagged it. The index found it.
"Who has thought hardest about transitional housing in our project?"
→ directory_query(topic: "transitional housing")
Reads the Context tensor. Routes to Pani — weighted by depth and recency. The directory function, computational.
Pre-Follow, three of these moves require interrupting someone, sitting in a meeting, or guessing. With Follow, they're a chat.
17 / 22
DEMO 2 · FINANCE + COMPLIANCE

Same architecture. Higher stakes.

Scenario: A finance team using ChatGPT and a compliance team using Claude share one Follow index — with governance boundaries that let both see what's contested at the boundary.
FINANCE
USING CHATGPT
"Has anything changed about our risk threshold position?"
→ query_index(freshness: include_superseded)
Threshold moved from one number to another on Monday. Source: a market analysis published the same week.
"Show me what compliance has touched recently — confidence above 0.85."
→ query_index(scope: layered)
Filtered cleanly. No leakage. Auditable.
COMPLIANCE
USING CLAUDE
"Show me anything contested between finance's recent positions and what we've signed off on."
→ detect_contradictions(cross_team: true)
Cross-team contradiction surfaces. The new finance threshold sits outside the bound compliance approved two weeks ago. Neither team raised it. The index saw both.
Architecture identical to Demo 1. The consequence of a missed contradiction is different — an unproductive meeting, or an audit finding six months later.
