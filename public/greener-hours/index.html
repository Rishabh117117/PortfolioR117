§ 14 · Precedents
14 / 17
The pattern · across domains

Greener Hours is not the first attempt.

REGULATORY DISCLOSURE · CONSUMER-FACING

EU Energy Labels

The A–G grade reshaped manufacturer behavior more than consumer behavior. Appliances were re-engineered to climb the visible scale.

FOOD INDUSTRY · MANDATORY DISCLOSURE

FDA Nutrition Labels

Did not change consumer behavior dramatically. Did drive food reformulation — trans fats, sodium, sugar — because manufacturers competed on what was visible.

CLOUD COMPUTE · ALREADY HAPPENED

AWS, Azure, GCP carbon dashboards

Launched 2021–2022 in response to enterprise procurement demand. The pattern works. The AI-specific extension is what's missing.

OBSERVABILITY · STANDARDS-DRIVEN

OpenTelemetry

Open backend standard spawned an entire UI ecosystem — Datadog, Grafana, Honeycomb. None of them exist without the substrate.

SOFTWARE SECURITY · IN PROGRESS

Software Bill of Materials

Federal procurement drives software supply chain disclosure. Procurement as adoption mechanism — same shape as Greener Hours.

AI · ADJACENT BUT INCOMPLETE

Green Software Foundation SCI

The Software Carbon Intensity specification (ISO/IEC 21031). Methodology exists. Productization & AI-specific extension do not.

§ 15 · Trade-offs & tensions
15 / 17
Named, not hidden

What this project cannot pretend.

P1 ↔ P9 · the structural tension

The biggest lever for AI's climate footprint is supply-side: data center siting, PPAs, grid decarbonization. Greener Hours operates on a derivative lever — user-facing legibility pressuring the supply side. The bet is contestable. The HTTPS analog suggests the pattern works; the timeline is uncertain.

Greenwashing capture risk

Open standards get captured. Three guardrails: open-source reference implementation, third-party verification of disclosure claims, anti-rebound layer surfacing total consumption alongside efficiency. None is sufficient alone.

Visibility ≠ behavior change

Sharpest midterm critique. Addressed structurally: Tier 1 is not a behavior tool. Behavior change happens at Tier 2 (one-click default) and Tier 3 (institutional decisions). Tier 1's job is legibility that creates supply-side pressure.

The sufficiency limit

Greener Hours is a timing and disclosure intervention, not a sufficiency intervention. It does not advocate for using less AI. Tier 3's total-consumption layer makes absolute usage visible — but visibility is not advocacy.

Honest limits · credibility through naming SP26 · May 12, 2026
